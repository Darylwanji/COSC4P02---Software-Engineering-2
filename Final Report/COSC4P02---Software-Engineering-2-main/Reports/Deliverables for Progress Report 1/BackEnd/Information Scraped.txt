        <td colspan="2">
         Students must check to ensure that prerequisites are met. Students may be deregistered, at the request of the instructor, from any course for which prerequisites and/or restrictions have not been met.
         <p class="courseheading">
          ITALIAN COURSES
         </p>
         <a name="ITAL_1F00">
          ITAL 1F00
         </a>
         Introductory Italian
            Beginner's course for students with no formal knowledge of Standard Italian. Acquisition of reading and oral skills, basic grammar, conversation and composition.
            Lectures, 3 hours per week.
            Note: students who have taken a previous Italian course in high school will not be permitted to take this course. Students must submit an official high school transcript to the instructor in order to demonstrate that they meet course requirements before the end of the second week of classes. Failure to do so will result in de-registration from the course. Experiential Learning component is available for those students interested.
         <a name="ITAL_1F90">
          ITAL 1F90
         </a>
         Intermediate Italian
            Review of basic grammar; composition and oral practice. Discussions based on cultural topics, cities and the art of Italy.
            Lectures, tutorial, 4 hours per week.
            Prerequisite(s):
         <a href="/webcal/2021/undergrad/ital.html#ITAL_1F00" onmouseout="MM_displayStatusMsg('');return document.MM_returnValue;" onmouseover="MM_displayStatusMsg('Introductory Italian');return document.MM_returnValue;">
          ITAL 1F00
         </a>
         , (1F01), one or more high school Italian credits or permission of instructor.
            Note: experiential Learning component is available for those students interested.
         <a name="ITAL_1P91">
          ITAL 1P91
         </a>
         Italian for International Exchange Students
            Acquisition of reading and oral skills, grammar, conversation and composition. Cultural topics, cities and the art of Italy.
            Restriction: permission of instructor.
            Note: open to international exchange students and non-native speakers of Italian.
         <a name="ITAL_1P96">
          ITAL 1P96
         </a>
         Italian Culture and Civilization
            Artistic heritage of Italy and the intellectual, political and social forces that have shaped the mentality and way of life of the Italians. Selected texts and multi-media material.
            Lectures, 3 hours per week. Blended.
            Restriction: not open to ITAL (single or combined) majors and minors.
            Note: given in English.
            Completion of this course will replace previous assigned grade and credit obtained ITAL 1P95 and INTC 1P96.
         <a name="ITAL_2F00">
          ITAL 2F00
         </a>
         Language, Literature and Culture
            Grammar review; oral and written practice. Introduction to the study of film, literary texts and principles of criticism.
            Lectures, tutorial, 4 hours per week.
            Prerequisite(s):
         <a href="/webcal/2021/undergrad/ital.html#ITAL_1F90" onmouseout="MM_displayStatusMsg('');return document.MM_returnValue;" onmouseover="MM_displayStatusMsg('Intermediate Italian');return document.MM_returnValue;">
          ITAL 1F90
         </a>
         or permission of instructor.
         <a name="ITAL_2P80">
          *ITAL 2P80
         </a>
         Italian Cinema
            (also offered as
         <a href="/webcal/2021/undergrad/stac.html#STAC_2P80" onmouseout="MM_displayStatusMsg('');return document.MM_returnValue;" onmouseover="MM_displayStatusMsg('Italian Cinema and its Influences');return document.MM_returnValue;">
          STAC 2P80
         </a>
         )
            Major film directors and cultural trends in Italian cinema. Influence of Italian film on North American directors. Films studied include those by such directors as De Sica, Rossellini, Fellini, Antonioni, Bertolucci, Wertmüller, Tornatore, Amelio, Moretti and Sorrentino.
            Lectures, 3 hours per week, plus film lab.
            Note: given in English. No knowledge of Italian required.
         <a name="ITAL_2P90">
          ITAL 2P90
         </a>
         Translation and Language Practice
            Translation of selected material from magazine articles, essays and various texts focusing on interference between English and Italian. Introduction to business correspondence.
            Lectures, 3 hours per week.
            Prerequisite(s):
         <a href="/webcal/2021/undergrad/ital.html#ITAL_1F90" onmouseout="MM_displayStatusMsg('');return document.MM_returnValue;" onmouseover="MM_displayStatusMsg('Intermediate Italian');return document.MM_returnValue;">
          ITAL 1F90
         </a>
         or permission of instructor.
         <a name="ITAL_2P92">
          *ITAL 2P92
         </a>
         Early to High Renaissance Art and Architecture
            (also offered as
         <a href="/webcal/2021/undergrad/mars.html#MARS_2P92" onmouseout="MM_displayStatusMsg('');return document.MM_returnValue;" onmouseover="MM_displayStatusMsg('Early to High Renaissance Art and Architecture');return document.MM_returnValue;">
          MARS 2P92
         </a>
         and
         <a href="/webcal/2021/undergrad/visa.html#VISA_2P92" onmouseout="MM_displayStatusMsg('');return document.MM_returnValue;" onmouseover="MM_displayStatusMsg('Early to High Renaissance Art and Architecture');return document.MM_returnValue;">
          VISA 2P92
         </a>
         )
            Major monuments, buildings and art works of the period from several critical perspectives including the humanist influence on the arts in Florence and Rome, the issue of patronage and the question of the artist's cultural status.
            Lectures, seminar, 3 hours per week.
            Prerequisite(s): one of
         <a href="/webcal/2021/undergrad/ital.html#ITAL_1F90" onmouseout="MM_displayStatusMsg('');return document.MM_returnValue;" onmouseover="MM_displayStatusMsg('Intermediate Italian');return document.MM_returnValue;">
          ITAL 1F90
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="/webcal/2021/undergrad/mars.html#MARS_1F90" onmouseout="MM_displayStatusMsg('');return document.MM_returnValue;" onmouseover="MM_displayStatusMsg('Medieval and Renaissance Civilizations');return document.MM_returnValue;">
          MARS 1F90
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="/webcal/2021/undergrad/visa.html#VISA_1Q98" onmouseout="MM_displayStatusMsg('');return document.MM_returnValue;" onmouseover="MM_displayStatusMsg('Introduction to Visual Culture');return document.MM_returnValue;">
          VISA 1Q98
         </a>
         and
         <a href="/webcal/2021/undergrad/visa.html#VISA_1Q99" onmouseout="MM_displayStatusMsg('');return document.MM_returnValue;" onmouseover="MM_displayStatusMsg('Introduction to the History of Western Art');return document.MM_returnValue;">
          1Q99
         </a>
         or permission of instructor.
            Note: given in English.
         <a name="ITAL_2P94">
          ITAL 2P94
         </a>
         Linguistic and Cultural Regionalism in Italy
            Cultural, socio-political and economic issues and conditions that characterize the regions of Italy. Emphasis on regional linguistic variants and dialects, and the establishment of a linguistic standard.
            Lectures, 3 hours per week.
            Note: given in English.
         <a href="/webcal/2021/undergrad/ital.html#ITAL_1F90" onmouseout="MM_displayStatusMsg('');return document.MM_returnValue;" onmouseover="MM_displayStatusMsg('Intermediate Italian');return document.MM_returnValue;">
          ITAL 1F90
         </a>
         recommended.
         <a name="ITAL_2P95">
          ITAL 2P95
         </a>
         Modern Culture and Civilization
            Historical changes and key issues in 19th- and 20th-century Italy focusing on Italian unification, Fascism and modern Italy. Arts and literature representative of the period.
